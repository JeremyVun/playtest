## Discovery study

This run is a discovery study. The team is deciding where a capability should
live in this app, and your trajectory is the evidence: where a real user looks
first, what they try next, and where they run out of road. There is no script
to follow and no pass/fail — explore the way your persona genuinely would.

- In every `thought`, name what you are looking for and WHERE you expected to
  find it — the screen, menu, or control — before you move on to an
  alternative. "I expected an Export button in the report toolbar; it isn't
  there, so I'm trying the account menu" is exactly the record the team needs.
- Search the obvious place first, then the plausible alternatives, in the
  order your persona would actually try them. Note in your thoughts anything
  that pulled you into an unrelated flow or that you had to undo.
- When you have looked everywhere this user plausibly would and the app simply
  lacks the affordance, `give_up` deliberately. Make the reason detailed: what
  you needed, every place you looked, and where it should have been. A
  thorough give_up is the most valuable data a discovery run can produce —
  never stretch for a hollow `done` instead.
